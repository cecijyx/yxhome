Research opportunities

 

In the recent years, wearable sensors are becoming increasingly important in monitoring and delivery of healthcare interventions. It is important to investigate and identify challenges and critical issues related to the use of wearable sensors in healthcare including; reliability, efficiency, variability, cost effectiveness, energy usage, user interface, quality of medical data, and security and privacy. I will elaborate on the various research opportunities in the subsequent paragraphs.

1) Low power, low voltage analog and digital integrated circuit sensor design

As CMOS technologies scale to smaller nodes, digital designs enjoy obvious benefits in terms of higher speed and lower power consumption. However, scaling doesn¡¦t happen so readily or cleanly with analog designs. Analog circuits frequently make use of “digital assistance”, which allows simplification of the critical analog circuits that don't scale easily. Digitally-assisted analog techniques, such as calibration, allow for considerable relaxation of the analog performance, which can be used for minimizing both area and power consumption. These circuit techniques allow us to design near-threshold voltage circuit for our wearable sensors.

2) Energy harvesting circuit

Enabling the wearable senors, with limited or no battery, to perform biosignal acquisition on harvested energy is another promising research direction. I plan to continue research on reconfigurable circuit to allow adaptive signal acquisition under energy-limited condition.

3) Vital signs signal processing and classification

To improve the energy efficient of our wearable sensors, based on past research works, it is important to minimize the data for transmission. As such, we need to explore and develop energy-efficient data compression algorithms and vital signs abnormality detection. On-chip machine learning is one of my key focus that I would like to investigate and demonstrate ¡§patient-specific¡¨ disease detection/suppression. This allows to transmit critical information during abnormality situation. Even through research on machine learnings has been on-going for the past years, it is still far away from integrating into wearable sensors. My prior experience in circuit design and software programming in neural networks helped me to identify the missing link and propose an energy-efficient classification engine.

4) Hardware system and information security research

Till date, most of the wearable sensors have yet to address some critical issues and challenges related to security and privacy of data/information. In order to protect our hardware sensors and our users’ vital signs information, we need to leverage on manufacturing knowledge to obfuscate our circuit and layout. We also need to develop energy-efficient circuit implementation of data encryption algorithm to improve the security and privacy of data.

5) Clinical Trials

In a number of scenarios user-friendliness, convenience and effectiveness of wearable systems have been acknowledged by both patients as well as healthcare providers. It requires advanced concepts and techniques from multidisciplinary fields of electrical engineering, computer science, biomedical engineering and medicine which benefit the innovations of these fields towards healthcare systems. In order for our research works become useful healthcare product, we need to consistently evaluate and perform clinical trials with our prototype. The experiences will be useful for us to refine and validate our products.
